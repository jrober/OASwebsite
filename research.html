Research

Active Research

We are currently developing a suite of solar devices to be proliferated throughout the BYU community. From this, we aim to answer several questions, such as:

  1. When a solar charging station is placed in a specific location, how much energy is used by the public?
  2. Is the device economical when placed in these locations?
  3. How much energy could be saved if the solar devices were proliferated throughout the nation? 

Action Steps

  • Test solar devices in various locations
    • Log the power being drawn by the user
    • Log the maximum power output of the panel
    • Ask the user to take a survey
  • Determine how economical the solar devices are when placed in various locations
    • Take the difference of the power used to the maximum power of the panels
      • If there is a large difference, smaller panels and batteries can be used to save on cost
    • Calculate the amount of money saved using the energy consumed, the price of the solar device, and standard energy costs
    • Determine how much energy could be saved if devices were proliferated throughout the country
  • In conjunction with application experiments on solar technologies we are experimenting on new and improved designs that can be conveniently fit to any situation. These designs are intended to be a one size fits all product. The idea is to create an easily installed device that can be conveniently modified to fit any size and shape while maintaining durability and efficiency.
